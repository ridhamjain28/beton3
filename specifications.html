Lighting Beyond Eternity

Beton develops indoor lighting fixtures engineered for precision, consistency, and long-term reliability. The collection is designed to support clear specification, predictable installation, and stable performance across interior environments.

Rather than relying on visual appeal alone, Beton focuses on measurable performance—controlled optics, managed glare, and defined color behaviour—so lighting performs as intended throughout its service life.

Each product is developed with attention to system compatibility, installation conditions, and real-world operating parameters. Documentation is structured to reduce ambiguity during design, coordination, and execution, allowing lighting decisions to be made with confidence.

Colour Rendering Index (CRI)

CRI measures the ability of a light source to reveal colours faithfully compared to a reference illuminant. High CRI values are critical for material-rich environments where surface perception impacts spatial quality.

TM-30 Colour Performance

TM-30 evaluates both fidelity (Rf) and saturation (Rg) for comprehensive colour analysis. Unlike single-metric ratings, it provides insight into how specific hues are rendered, ensuring balanced visual results.

Correlated Colour Temperature (CCT)

CCT defines the perceived warmth or coolness of the luminous environment. Consistent selection and strict binning ensure visual harmony across adjacent architectural zones and fixture types.

Chromatic Stability (MacAdam)

MacAdam ellipses define the consistency between individual LED chips. Tight step tolerances (SDCM < 3) prevent visible colour shifts in linear arrays or wall-washing applications.

Unified Glare Rating (UGR)

UGR quantifies the risk of visual discomfort caused by high-luminance sources. Low UGR optics are essential for maintaining occupant comfort and productivity in workspaces.

Tunable White Control

DYNAMIC CONTROL CURVE

Tunable white technology enables independent adjustment of colour temperature and intensity. This capability supports circadian entrainment and adapts the luminous environment to varying functional requirements.

Lifetime Colour Performance

L80B10 / 50,000H

Lumen maintenance metrics (L80B10) predict light output retention and colour stability over time. Reliable performance data supports accurate lifecycle planning and maintenance scheduling.

Lighting Performance & Control

Lighting quality is influenced by optical design, thermal stability, and component consistency. Beton fixtures are specified using defined performance parameters to support reliable output, visual comfort, and long-term stability.

Luminous Flux (lm)

OUTPUT

Total visible light emitted by a light source.

Fixture Efficiency

RATIO

Ratio of usable light exiting the fixture to the light generated by the source.

Illuminance (lux)

LEVELS

Amount of luminous flux received on a surface, used to define lighting levels within a space.

Luminous Efficacy (lm/W)

EFFICIENCY

Efficiency of converting electrical input into visible light, supporting energy-conscious design decisions.

Control & Dimming Options

Lighting control influences visual comfort, energy use, and adaptability over time. Beton fixtures are designed to integrate reliably with commonly used control systems, with compatibility defined clearly at the product level.

Digital Control

DALI / DMX

Digital lighting control enables centralized management, scene setting, and integration with building systems. Suitable for projects requiring coordinated control across multiple fixtures or zones.

Phase-Cut Dimming

MAINS DIM

Compatible with leading-edge or trailing-edge dimmers, depending on driver configuration. Used where retrofit compatibility or simple user control is required.

Integrated Dimming

ON-BOARD

Certain fixtures incorporate on-board dimming for direct, localized control without external interfaces.

Analog Dimming (1-10V)

1-10V

Provides continuous dimming through a low-voltage control signal. Widely used for its simplicity, reliability, and compatibility with standard control infrastructure.

Control Coordination

Dimming behaviour, minimum dim levels, and control compatibility depend on the selected driver and configuration. These parameters are specified per product to support accurate coordination during design and installation. Clear control documentation reduces commissioning issues and ensures the intended lighting performance is achieved in use.
